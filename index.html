How HandAuth Pro Works

A structured four-step process designed to integrate into professional handwriting examination workflows.

01

Sample Submission

A handwriting sample or document image is submitted to the system. No files are stored on external servers — the workflow remains within the operator's controlled environment.

02

Characteristic Extraction

The software extracts key handwriting characteristics: stroke structure, letter formation, proportions, spacing, pen-lift points, and baseline alignment.

03

Comparative Analysis

Advanced comparison algorithms analyze structural similarities and deviations between the submitted sample and reference material, identifying pattern inconsistencies.

04

Indicator Report

The system generates a structured report containing analytical indicators and a probability score — designed to assist expert interpretation, not to replace it.

Background: Digital and Classical Methods

A brief educational note on how automated tools, cryptographic methods, and traditional handwriting examination differ — and why human expert review remains essential.

Overview of digital signatures, hashing, and the limits of automated screening vs. classical forensic examination.

Digital signatures and secure hashing can verify that a digital file has not been altered since it was created, and can anchor a scan of a handwritten sample to an auditable record. Automated AI screening, like HandAuth Pro, looks for broad statistical patterns and obvious artifacts. However, classical forensic examination by a qualified document examiner goes much further — evaluating stroke order, pen pressure, rhythm, tremor, and microscopic characteristics that automated tools cannot reliably assess. For reports and internal records, HandAuth Pro can produce a signed manifest recording the image hash and basic metadata. This supports file integrity checking, but it is not equivalent to a certified forensic opinion.

Educational Materials & FAQ

General educational information about signature screening, what automated tools can and cannot detect, and when to seek a certified forensic document examiner. None of the following constitutes legal advice or an expert conclusion.

How to provide good reference signature samples

Improving sample quality for preliminary screening.

Providing clear, consistent reference signature images improves the quality of any preliminary automated check. Capture flat, well-lit scans with no shadows, note any variation in signing conditions, and avoid cropping away pen strokes. Better input leads to more meaningful preliminary signals — but quality references do not make automated output equivalent to a certified expert opinion.

⚠ This is general educational information only. Not legal advice or an expert conclusion.

Understanding preliminary screening scores

What orientational probability outputs mean — and what they don't.

Automated screening scores are orientational only. A low-risk score does not confirm authenticity; a high-risk score does not prove forgery. Scores indicate which cases may warrant closer attention and professional review. Always treat automated outputs as triage signals, never as conclusions. Any case with legal significance should be referred to a certified forensic document examiner.

⚠ This is general educational information only. Not legal advice or an expert conclusion.

What automated tools can detect — and their limits

Indicators, visualisations, and important caveats.

Automated AI screening can flag obvious non-handwritten artifacts (prints, screen captures, synthetic patterns) and broad statistical anomalies. However, subtle skilled forgeries, disguised handwriting, and many borderline cases require experienced human judgment. Visualisations and heuristic scores are educational aids — they do not constitute forensic analysis.

⚠ This is general educational information only. Not legal advice or an expert conclusion.

When should I hire a certified forensic document examiner?

Understanding when preliminary screening is not enough.

You should always engage a certified forensic document examiner when: the matter involves legal proceedings, contracts, inheritance, fraud allegations, or regulatory compliance; when preliminary screening returns borderline or suspicious results; or when the consequences of an error are significant. Preliminary AI tools like HandAuth Pro are a starting point, not a final answer.

⚠ This is general educational information only. Not legal advice or an expert conclusion.

Why image quality affects preliminary screening

Better inputs, more reliable early signals.

Clear, high-resolution scans help reduce false positives in automated screening. Blurry, low-contrast, or heavily compressed images limit what any algorithm can detect. Improving capture quality gives you more meaningful preliminary signals — while remembering that even perfect images cannot make automated output equivalent to certified expert analysis.

⚠ This is general educational information only. Not legal advice or an expert conclusion.

Key takeaway from all of the above.

Preliminary AI screening is a valuable first step that can save time and money by flagging cases worth closer investigation. However, it has real limitations: automated tools cannot reliably assess subtle skilled forgeries, cannot consider context, and cannot produce court-admissible findings. Always supplement preliminary screening with professional human review for any matter that carries legal, financial, or reputational risk.
